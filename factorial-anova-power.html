Confidence Level

The probability of correctly concluding that a factor has no effect, when it truly doesn't. This is your protection against declaring a false discovery.

Power

The probability of correctly detecting that a factor has an effect, when it truly does. This is the sensitivity of your experiment to find a real result.

Type I Error (α, alpha)

The error of concluding that a factor is significant when it actually has no effect on the outcome. This is a 'false positive' or a spurious finding.

Type II Error (β, beta)

The error of failing to conclude that a factor is significant when it actually does have an effect. This is a 'false negative' or a missed opportunity to find a real discovery.
